Kenzan:

At Kenzan, we create elegant software solutions that capture the eye and imagination. We pride ourselves on our kind and collaborative approach to difficult problems. If you are interested in joining our community of passionate and creative people, we’d like to hear from you. We’re now accepting applications for paid apprenticeships in our Kenzan offices starting Summer 2018. We need people who are innovative, detail-oriented, and relish the opportunity to work on a broad range of projects with a diverse team of smart and quirky individuals. If you are just starting your career and excited to build your experience with the Kenzan community, please submit your resume and contact information and we’ll reach out for further conversation.

Program Expectations:

Apprentices will work at Kenzan’s Providence or Denver office over the course of 10 weeks for 30 hours per week starting in June 2018. You will receive professional soft skills and hard skills trainings, be matched with an experienced staff mentor, and be included in Kenzan’s regular community events such as Hack Nights and Lunch & Learns. An apprenticeship at Kenzan means working with a team of top developers on projects that align with our leadership’s strategic vision and will move Kenzan forward as a leader in software development.

Utilidata:

Utilidata is a Providence-based software analytics company (>30 employees) focused on modernizing the electric grid. For nearly decade, we’ve partnered with many of the largest utilities in the US to optimize power delivery through real-time coordinated control of grid operators’ critical equipment. With Utilidata’s Volt/VAR Optimization (VVO) software, utilities gain powerful visibility into their operations while driving significant customer cost savings and energy efficiency. Utilidata is the market leader for this technology, which the Environmental Defense Fund describes as “one of the lowest of the low-hanging fruits that could accelerate the transition to a clean energy economy”.

The convergence of several global trends in energy and transportation is increasing the need for powerful grid analytics and control software:

  • Distributed energy resources (DERs): A growing number of rooftop solar panels, distributed wind turbines, fuel cells, and batteries are upending the traditional grid model (i.e., large central power plants, one-way power flows) and demanding new software tools.
  • Electric vehicles (EVs): Current projections for US EV sales in the coming decade far exceed what our grid infrastructure can support. Utilities require novel analytics capabilities to ensure continued grid stability and reliability even as EVs proliferate.
  • Cyber-physical security: Increasingly networked power infrastructure represents a uniquely vulnerable target for cyber attackers, as evidenced by recent reports of breaches by Russian hackers. Real-time operational visibility and anomaly detection are crucial elements of utilities’ cyber defense.

Utilidata is seeking a computer science intern to work alongside our data science and software development teams to build the next generation of energy analytics applications. Candidates should be excited about energy technology, smart city/infrastructure, next-gen transportation, and/or sustainability.

Qualifications:

  • Software application development for programmable embedded computing systems
  • Advanced statistical methods / algorithms (e.g., probabilistic methods, optimization, machine learning, visualization)
  • Some familiarity with electrical engineering or power systems is considered ‘a plus’, though not required
